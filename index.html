Understanding the Stock Market

Stock market charts showing price movement

The stock market is a system where shares of companies are bought and sold.

It allows companies to raise money for expansion, innovation, and operations.

People who buy shares are called investors or shareholders.

Each share represents partial ownership in a company.

BSE and NSE represent India’s stock markets

In India, major stock exchanges are the BSE and NSE.

Sensex and Nifty are important indices that track market performance.

Stock prices change based on demand, supply, and company results.

Bull market – rising prices and optimism

When prices rise steadily, it is known as a bull market.

Bear market – falling prices and fear

When prices fall for a long time, it is called a bear market.

Companies raise funds for the first time through an Initial Public Offering.

Investors earn money through capital gains and dividends.

Market capitalization shows how large a company is.

The P/E ratio helps judge whether a stock is overvalued or undervalued.

Trading volume shows how actively a stock is traded.

Volatility measures how sharply prices move.

Higher returns usually involve higher risk.

Overall, the stock market helps businesses grow and investors build wealth.
